Safe Water, Good Hygiene, Better Health

Understanding water, hygiene, and sanitation practices is essential for preventing disease and improving quality of life worldwide.

Learn More

Water

Clean and safe drinking water is critical to human survival. Access to safe water reduces the risk of diarrheal diseases and promotes healthier communities.

Hygiene

Good hygiene practices such as handwashing with soap help stop the spread of bacteria and viruses. Hygiene education is a cornerstone of public health.

Sanitation

Proper sanitation facilities and waste management systems are essential for reducing exposure to harmful pathogens and improving overall well-being.

Hygiene, Sanitation & Health Risks

Practice Good Outcome Risk if Ignored
Handwashing with soap Reduces spread of infections Increased risk of diarrhea, cholera
Safe water storage Maintains water quality Contamination, typhoid, dysentery
Proper waste disposal Clean surroundings, safe environment Vector-borne diseases, pollution
Use of latrines Improves community sanitation Open defecation, parasitic infections
